Exoplanet PSR B1257+12 b

Overview

PSR B1257+12 b, also known as Draugr, is the first confirmed exoplanet discovered outside our solar system. It orbits the pulsar PSR B1257+12, located approximately 980 light-years away in the constellation Virgo.

Discovery

The planet was discovered in 1992 by astronomers Aleksander Wolszczan and Dale A. Frail. This groundbreaking discovery opened the door to the exploration of exoplanets, leading to the identification of thousands of such worlds in the years that followed.

Characteristics

  • Type: Rocky planet
  • Mass: Approximately 4.3 times that of Earth
  • Orbital Period: About 66 days
  • Distance from Pulsar: Approximately 0.36 AU
