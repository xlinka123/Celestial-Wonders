Exoplanet

Exoplanet

Exoplanets are planets that are located outside of our solar system. Exoplanets are notable for various features, such as being in their planet's "goldilocks zone," a zone a planet can be in in relation to its star that makes it have potential for life. Featured exoplanets include K218b and PSR B1257+12b.

Black Holes

Black Hole

Black holes are regions in space where the gravitational pull is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape from them. They are formed from the remnants of massive stars after they collapse. For more information, check out our page on Ton 618, one of the largest known black holes.

Stars

Star

Stars are massive celestial bodies made of gas that emit light and heat from nuclear fusion reactions in their cores. They come in various sizes, colors, and stages of life. Explore the fascinating characteristics of stars like Sirius B, a well-known binary star system.

Galaxies

Galaxy

Galaxies are vast systems that contain millions to trillions of stars, along with gas, dust, and dark matter. They come in various shapes and sizes, including spiral, elliptical, and irregular galaxies. Learn more about the massive galaxy IC 1101, one of the largest known galaxies in the universe.
